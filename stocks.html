Paranet Stocks Information

Paranet is a startup project that has not yet been registered as a formal company, though steps are actively being taken to complete the registration process. The platform itself is fully developed and is preparing for test public launch of version one on June 5, 2025.

At present, the estimated valuation of Paranet stands at $250,000, based on the development progress, strategic potential, and early-stage groundwork. By the time of country wide launch in fall 2025, the valuation is projected to reach or even exceed $450,000. We have allocated a total of 10 million private shares, which are not publicly traded and are offered only to key participants who contribute to the growth and success of the platform.

These shares are strictly reserved for:

Co-founders
Developers as Employees
Angel Investors
Advisors

We currently do not offer any shares to the general public or retail investors.

Equity Options

To support and reward our collaborators, we offer two distinct types of equity arrangements. Each is tailored to suit the different kinds of partnerships we are building.

RSUs (Restricted Stock Units)

RSUs are intended for employees or developers who want real equity in the form of company shares. Under this approach, each eligible developer may receive 1.2% of the total shares, which equals 120,000 shares. These are actual shares that represent ownership in Paranet, and they are granted progressively after completing a vesting period defined in the contract.

Although RSU holders gain equity, voting rights are limited. Only individuals who hold 10% or more of the company’s total shares are granted voting privileges. Voting power is primarily reserved for co-founders, major investors, and strategic advisors. Final decisions for the company remain with the founder. Additionally, if an RSU holder wishes to sell their shares in the future, they are contractually obligated to offer them first to current shareholders before selling them externally. This is a protective measure to maintain the integrity of the ownership structure.

SARs (Stock Appreciation Rights)

SARs are designed for contributors who prefer not to take ownership but still want to benefit from the company’s growth. This includes certain co-founders, developers, advisors, and angel investors.

Under the SAR model, no actual shares are issued. Instead, participants receive a contractual right to receive a cash-equivalent value of their assigned percentage based on the company’s increase in valuation. SAR holders are not required to undergo a vesting period, but they must remain engaged with the company until it reaches a $450,000 valuation, at which point they may exit with their entitled payout.

The SAR distribution is defined as follows:

Developers (as employees): eligible for up to 3.5% of total SAR value
Co-founders: eligible for up to 15%
Angel Investors: eligible for up to 14%
Advisors: eligible for up to 5%
Venture Capitalists are excluded from SAR offerings due to their usual requirement for equity ownership
